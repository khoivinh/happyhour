Khlock wins decisively — highest average by a wide margin (4.29 vs. 2.79 for second place), 8 out of 14 voters gave it a 5, and nobody rated it below 3.

## Analysis

**Khlock** is the only name that achieved consensus. It's not just that it averaged highest — it had zero scores below 3 and eight scores of 5. The other three names all had significant resistance (multiple 1s or 2s). Voters clearly feel the current name already works.

**Goldenhour** came in second (2.79 avg) but the scores are polarized — it got two 5s (Laura Holder and jj, with jj calling it "aces") but also two 1s. It's a love-it-or-hate-it name. The people who liked it seemed drawn to the evocative, warm quality, but most voters landed in the lukewarm 2-3 range. It doesn't have a strong middle — it either resonates or it doesn't.

**Hi Time** is the flattest performer (2.71 avg). No one gave it a 5, and its scores cluster tightly around 2-4. It's nobody's favorite and nobody's least favorite — the kind of name that doesn't offend but also doesn't stick. For an app name, that indifference is arguably worse than polarization, since you want something memorable.

**Tymely** was the weakest candidate (2.21 avg) with five scores of 1 — the most rejection of any name. Only Jon Rodriguez rated it a 5. The misspelling that was meant to make it ownable may have worked against it.
